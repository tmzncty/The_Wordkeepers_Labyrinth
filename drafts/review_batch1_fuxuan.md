## Executive Summary

The five chapters are **structurally sound** and **emotionally effective**. Feimo's entry into the Labyrinth, the tsundere Mia dynamic, and the thematic throughline about language preservation are all working. The knowledge cards (10 total) are naturally embedded.

However, there are **three P0 issues** that must be fixed before the drafts can advance:

1. **Naming inconsistency across all major characters** — each character has 2+ different names across chapters
2. **Massive content repetition** — the same passages, theories, and exposition appear 2-3 times across the five chapters
3. **Mia's vulnerability too early** — her full backstory reveal in Ch.2 breaks the tsundere shell before Ch.5

The filler problem is moderate — roughly 15-20% of each chapter is redundant internal reflection or repeated exposition.

## Chapter 1: The Cat-Ear Girl and the Cruelest Spirit

**Lines**: 326 | **Est. Words**: ~5,500 | **Verdict**: ⚠️ Conditional Pass (2 P1s, 3 P2s)

### ✅ What Works
- Opening hook is strong — "The textbook was talking to her" in 3 words places us in the Labyrinth
- Mia's tsundere voice is pitch-perfect from line 1: "Congratulations on your first accurate observation of the evening"
- The mirror scene with ears/tail is the right mix of disorienting and charming
- Hawthorne cloze challenge is well-integrated as an in-world corridor test
- The Chinese knocking (Tap. Tap. Tap.) is properly mysterious; Mia's "Hear what?" denial is well-timed
- Bridge House dormitory scene lands the cat-warmth instinct naturally
- Returns to Study Room 4B ending is clean and satisfying

### Issues

#### P1 — L169: Forced Mirror-wing crack during Weave challenge

> *"A section of wall to Feimo's left cracked open, and through the crack she could see another corridor, this one lined with mirrors, a flash of silver-gray light. Somewhere in that parallel passage, someone was reading aloud, their voice carrying fragments of analysis about the decline of arts criticism..."*

**Problem**: This is an unmotivated narrative interruption. Feimo is doing a Weave challenge; suddenly a crack opens and she hears a Mirror passage mid-analysis? The Weave corridor is supposed to be unstable, but this reads like the author needed to introduce the arts criticism passage somewhere and wedged it in here. It also steals the thunder from Ch.3's Mirror House session.

**Fix**: Delete the crack entirely. The Weave challenge should focus on Hawthorne. The arts criticism passage belongs in Ch.3 only.

#### P1 — L211–218: Mia's Leopold exposition dump

> *"Those are Deep Archive echoes. Old text, fragments from ecological philosophy... Aldo Leopold. A Sand County Almanac. 1949..."*

**Problem**: Feimo reads wall fragments, and Mia immediately identifies the author, the source, the year, and the argument — in a monologue. This is exposition by AI assistant. It also front-loads the Leopold "intrinsic right" theme that should develop more gradually across Ch.1 and Ch.4.

**Fix**: Let Feimo *feel* the wall fragments without identification. She can trace the words, feel their weight, sense their significance — but NOT have Mia explain them yet. Save the Leopold identification for Ch.4's private lesson with Silent, where it has narrative purpose. This preserves mystery and prevents repetition.

#### P2 — L249–253: End-of-chapter reflection is over-explained

> *"She thought about the Hawthorne workers... She thought about the words on the wall... She thought about the knocking beneath the floor."*

**Problem**: Three consecutive "She thought about" paragraphs. The reader already lived these moments. This is narratorial hand-holding.

**Fix**: Cut to one sentence. Something like: *The knocking had not stopped. The words had not faded. And the Hawthorne workers, she suspected, would have understood.* Let the reader connect the dots.

#### P2 — L65: Mia's Four Houses explanation is a wall of text

Mia's exposition about the Four Houses is a single long paragraph. It reads like the worldbuilding doc pasted into dialogue.

**Fix**: Break it up. Let Feimo ask questions. Let Mia interrupt herself with snark. Make it a conversation, not a lecture.

#### P2 — L273: Bridge House description is dense

The multilingual walls, the cross-linguistic map, the couch description — all in one paragraph. Needs breathing room.

**Fix**: Spread the description across Feimo's movement through the room. Let her discover details one by one, with cat-instinct reactions (gravitating to warmth, ears tracking languages).

## Chapter 2: Forget to Remember

**Lines**: 354 | **Est. Words**: ~6,000 | **Verdict**: ❌ Needs Revision (2 P0s, 2 P1s, 2 P2s)

### ✅ What Works
- The opening metaphor — "Glyphs forgot you the way a coastline forgets a shipwreck" — is excellent
- SM-2 taught through the cat-territory metaphor is organic and memorable
- Moon/Yue's entrance is devastating: "I can't dream anymore" / "Some things are like that. They don't want to be fixed. They just want to be spoken."
- The star map scene is the emotional core and it earns its weight
- Mia's "I've had thirty-seven students" confession is powerful writing

### Issues

#### P0 — Naming: "Moon" vs "Yue"

L195: She introduces herself as *"I'm Moon."* But in Ch.3 she's consistently called "Yue" (L19: "Is Yue coming?"; L307: "Yue was in the corner"). The worldbuilding says "月 (Yue)."

**Fix**: Standardize to **Yue** across all chapters. If the character has an English-rendered name, it should be consistent. "Moon" introduces confusion. Use "Yue" everywhere; it matches the worldbuilding table.

#### P0 — Arts criticism passage appears TWICE (Ch.2 AND Ch.3)

Ch.2 lines 103–163: Mia teaches the arts criticism passage (Cardus, Shaw, Newman, "inexorable decline") with full comprehension questions.

Ch.3 lines 41–63: Mirror House senior teaches the **exact same passage** with the same details (Cardus knighted 1967, "unknown save to specialists," Shaw and Newman).

**Problem**: The reader encounters the same passage, the same critic biographical details, and the same thematic conclusions in back-to-back chapters. This is not foreshadowing or deepening — it's repetition. It makes the narrative feel padded.

**Fix**: **Remove the arts criticism passage from Ch.2 entirely.** Ch.2's Study Break should focus exclusively on SM-2 and the star map. The three-layer reading method (surface/structural/resonant) can be taught using a DIFFERENT passage — or the teaching can be deferred to Ch.6 (which is outlined as Study Break #2: Reading Strategy). Ch.2 already has enough content (SM-2 + Moon + star map). The reading passage analysis bloats it.

#### P1 — Three-layer reading method appears too early

The notes for Ch.2 list "课堂：三层阅读法" in the scene budget, but Study Break #1 is SM-2, not reading strategy. Study Break #2 (reading strategy layers) is scheduled for Ch.6. Having Mia teach the three-layer method in Ch.2 pre-empts Ch.6's teaching content.

**Fix**: If the three-layer method must appear in Ch.2, make it a brief mention — Mia says "We'll cover this properly later" — and save the full lesson for Ch.6. Or remove it from Ch.2 entirely and use the freed space for more Moon/Yue development.

#### P1 — L293–311: Mia's full backstory reveal is premature

> *"Before you, I had another student... I've had thirty-seven students, Feimo. Thirty-seven Pilgrims assigned to my guidance over the years. Three graduated with gold stars. Three out of thirty-seven."*

**Problem**: The notes for Ch.1 explicitly state: "不要过早让 Mia 变暖——她的外壳必须撑到 Ch.5." But in Ch.2, Mia reveals her entire history — 37 students, 34 lost, a frozen star she still watches. This is the deepest vulnerability in Phase 1, delivered in the second chapter. The tsundere shell isn't cracking; it's shattering.

The outline for Ch.12 says: *"Mia reveals sealed memories. 'I don't know who I was before.'"* — that's the intended big Mia reveal for Part I. If Ch.2 already gives us everything, Ch.12's reveal has no impact.

**Fix**: **Cut the backstory to a fragment.** Mia shows Feimo the star map. She points out the dark stars. She says something like: *"Most of those are mine. Students I was assigned to guide. They didn't make it."* And then she stops. She does NOT give the number 37. She does NOT tell the story of the specific student who left. She does NOT reveal the frozen star's identity. Save these for Ch.12. In Ch.2, Mia should show *hints* of sadness — a flicker, a pause, a catch in her voice — not a full confession.

#### P2 — L345–349: Final paragraph is over-written

The ending paragraph is 5+ lines of compound sentences restating what the reader already knows. "The dark stars outnumbered the bright ones" was established 90 lines earlier.

**Fix**: Cut to 3 sentences max. End on the SM-2 promise, not a recap of the star map.

#### P2 — L259: Feimo's reflection paragraph is redundant

She thinks about dark stars, Cardus, Hawthorne workers, two-step flow — all in one paragraph. This is a summary of everything that happened, for a reader who just read it.

**Fix**: Pick ONE thread and let it resonate. The Cardus connection is the strongest.

## Chapter 3: The Girl Who Read Too Much

**Lines**: 364 | **Est. Words**: ~6,000+ | **Verdict**: ⚠️ Conditional Pass (1 P0, 2 P1s, 2 P2s)

### ✅ What Works
- Aya's entrance is cinematic: "the quiet economy of someone who had eliminated every unnecessary gesture"
- "A cat girl. In Bridge House. What an insult to the Academy." — Perfect cold contempt
- The hidden passage discovery uses cat hearing organically
- Aya's "You are not an influential" speech reframes Feimo's ability correctly
- The scene of Aya standing in the corridor, not leaving, is the best character beat in the five chapters
- Yue's defamiliarization-as-loss speech is haunting

### Issues

#### P0 — L37: "Mirror House was exclusively human"

> *"Feimo saw no cat ears, no animal features of any kind. Mirror House was exclusively human, and despite the Academy's official commitment to diversity, the absence of demi-kind students felt less like coincidence and more like policy."*

**Problem**: The worldbuilding (§1.3) states: "Demi-kind are not oppressed or persecuted. The Academy is too old and too reliant on linguistic talent to care about ear shape." It also says the prejudice is "a prejudice of underestimation" — not segregation. Saying Mirror House is "exclusively human" and implying an exclusionary policy **contradicts** the worldbuilding. The world design makes a point of NOT having systemic discrimination; instead, there's a subtler prejudice of dismissal.

**Fix**: Change to something like: *"Feimo noticed fewer demi-kind students here than in Bridge House. The ones she saw had their features tucked away, minimized, as if the House culture encouraged blending in rather than standing out."* This preserves the sense that Mirror House is less welcoming to demi-kind without inventing a segregation policy the worldbuilding explicitly rejects.

#### P1 — Arts criticism passage repeated from Ch.2

Lines 41–63: The Mirror House session covers the same arts criticism passage that Ch.2 already analyzed. Same passage, same critics, same thematic conclusions.

**Fix**: If the Ch.2 repetition is removed (per the P0 fix above), this becomes the FIRST and only appearance of the passage, which is correct. If for some reason Ch.2 keeps the passage, then Ch.3 must use a DIFFERENT text for the Mirror House session.

#### P1 — L95–99 & L277: Aya's stats given twice

First occurrence (L95): Mia whispers Aya's stats during the Mirror House session.
Second occurrence (L277): Mia repeats the same stats when Feimo reports the hidden passage.

> *"Aya of Mirror House. First in her year. Continuous since enrollment. Her analytical scores are the highest the House has recorded in a decade..."*

**Fix**: First occurrence is fine. Second occurrence should be cut or replaced with a single line: *"I know who Aya is."* The reader doesn't need the CV twice.

#### P1 — L227–243: Aya's Gladwell lecture

Aya explains the two-step flow of communication theory in a monologue while standing in front of a hidden passage she just discovered. She cites Gladwell, Watts, Dodds, and the concept of "global cascades."

**Problem**: This is the author speaking through Aya, not Aya being Aya. Aya would not deliver a lecture in this context. She would be terse, clinical, dismissive. The theory is relevant, but the delivery breaks character.

**Fix**: Aya should make a single cutting remark — *"Your ears are not talent. They are a different angle. Subsequent research on social influence showed the same thing: it is not the exceptional individual that matters, but the readiness of the system to respond."* — and move on. Two sentences, not seventeen lines.

#### P2 — L71–79: Three-branch classification taught AGAIN

The formalist's three branches (theory/history/criticism) and Aristotle's three genres appear here, after already being covered in Ch.1's sorting ceremony and Ch.2's teaching.

**Fix**: Remove the three-branch repetition. Aya's senior can reference it in passing ("As you know from your foundational courses...") without re-explaining.

#### P2 — L295–297: Conference notice poster

A notice about an "International Conference on Inter-Labyrinth Exchange" appears on a bulletin board. It goes nowhere in this chapter.

**Fix**: Either delete it or commit to it appearing again within 2-3 chapters. Dead foreshadowing is worse than no foreshadowing.

**Lines**: 486 | **Est. Words**: ~8,000 | **Verdict**: ⚠️ Conditional Pass (1 P0, 2 P1s, 3 P2s)

### ✅ What Works

- The connotation/denotation lesson flows naturally from post-battle analysis
- Feimo studying after the battle — character-establishing

### Issues

#### P0 — Naming: "Noah" vs "Noir"

The entire chapter calls the character "Noah" (L81, 85, 87, etc.). The worldbuilding table says "Noir (诺亚)." The notes say "诺亚." This is the same inconsistency pattern as Aya/Ling and Moon/Yue.

**Fix**: Standardize. The worldbuilding uses "Noir." Either change all drafts to "Noir" or change the worldbuilding to "Noah." **Recommendation**: Use the names from the worldbuilding table (Noir, Aya, Yue, Professor Silence) as canonical and fix all drafts to match.

#### P1 — L5: "Silence Professor" vs "Professor Silent"

> *"Three days had passed since Silence Professor's wall had inclined forward"*

Ch.4 uses "Professor Silent." Ch.5 uses "Silence Professor." The Chinese is "缄默教授."

**Fix**: Standardize to "Professor Silent" (matches Ch.4's usage and is more natural in English).

#### P1 — L165: "Despite" classified as Adverb

> *"...The Glyph for 'despite' materialized in her hand, thin and white, an Adverb of concession."*

**Problem**: "Despite" is a **preposition**, not an adverb. The worldbuilding's POS table (§3.2) classifies prepositions under "Other" (2.1%): *"Prepositions, conjunctions, pronouns. Essential for chaining multiple Glyphs into complex sequences."* Calling "despite" an Adverb of Concession contradicts the Glyph type system.

Additionally, the worldbuilding says Adverbs are "the rarest and most prized Glyphs" at 1.9%. If common prepositions get relabeled as adverbs, the rarity system breaks.

**Fix**: Change to *"the Glyph for 'despite' materialized in her hand, thin and white, a Connector Glyph — a preposition, the binding agent between concepts."* This preserves the type system's integrity.

#### P2 — L25–39: Knocking conversation rehash

Mia and Feimo have the same exchange about the knocking for the third time:
- Ch.1: "Do you hear that?" / "Hear what?"
- Ch.4: "I can hear something" (implicit)
- Ch.5: "You're doing it again" / "Listening to things that aren't there"

Each time, Mia denies/deflects, and Feimo pushes. The pattern is established. Repeating it doesn't advance anything.

**Fix**: In Ch.5, cut the knocking exchange to 2-3 lines and use it as a transition, not a scene. Something like: *The knocking had returned. Feimo didn't bother telling Mia this time. She just followed it.*

#### P2 — L245: "yijing" appears without context

> *"...what scholars of her mother's tradition would call yijing — where emotion and scene fuse into something neither could be alone."*

**Problem**: "Her mother's tradition" is the first mention of Feimo's family/Chinese heritage. It's dropped mid-battle with no setup. Also, Card 007 (Yijing) is supposed to be embedded in this chapter — but "yijing" here means "artistic conception" (意境), not the *Yijing* (易经, Book of Changes). These are different concepts. The card title "Yijing" likely refers to the Book of Changes, not artistic conception.

**Fix**: Clarify which "yijing" is meant. If it's 意境 (artistic conception), rename the card. If it's 易经 (Book of Changes), the reference needs to be about divination/change, not emotion-scene fusion. Also, "her mother's tradition" needs earlier setup or should be cut in favor of something less specific.

#### P2 — Post-battle analysis section (L356–382) is long

The connotation/denotation lesson + narrative focalization + Noah's philosophy + the knocking conversation + Mia's monitoring admission — this is a lot of post-battle content. The reader has already had a 100-line battle scene. The analysis section adds another 80+ lines.

**Fix**: Split into two beats. The connotation lesson happens on the platform (keep). The narrative focalization discussion happens later, on the walk back (move to the staircase scene). This prevents info-dump stacking.

### P1 — Feimo's Competence Trajectory

The notes warn: "不要让绯墨表现得太强——她的战绩是 3-5."

Across Ch.1-5:
- Ch.1: Zero errors on Hawthorne cloze (unusually good for a first-year)
- Ch.2: Quickly grasps SM-2 and three-layer reading
- Ch.3: Discovers a passage Mirror House missed for centuries
- Ch.4: Wall bows to her translation (unprecedented in 4 years)
- Ch.5: Launches a Glyph constellation that stuns a second-year veteran

Feimo is being set up as exceptional. The 3-5 record mentioned in Ch.9 will feel contradictory if she keeps performing miracles. The narrative needs more moments of genuine failure or confusion to balance the wins.

**Suggestion**: In Ch.1, give her 1-2 errors on the Hawthorne cloze (not zero). In Ch.3, have the hidden passage discovery be more ambiguous — she feels *something* but can't identify it clearly until Aya confirms it. In Ch.5, the constellation is fine because she loses the battle — but make the loss feel less noble and more like a first-year running out of resources.
