---

### Q46. They sometimes travel more than sixty miles to find food or water, and are very good at working out where other elephants are—even when they are out of sight.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句结构简单，主干为They travel and are good at working out…，注意miles的翻译，可以翻译为“英里”，也可以翻译为“路程”。“work out”在这里的意思是“弄明白，计算出”。

---

### Q47. The researchers are convinced that the elephants always know precisely where they are in relation to all the resources they need, and can therefore take shortcuts, as well as following familiar routes.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句的主干是The researchers are convinced that…，that引导的是宾语从句。In relation to…是一个常见的介词短语，表示“相对于…”。as well as 连接两个并列的动词短语，following familiar routes.

---

### Q48. One possibility was that they merely used their eyes and tried out the plants they found, but that would probably result in a lot of wasted time and energy, not least because their eyesight is actually not very good.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句是一个复合句，由一个主句和一个but引导的转折分句构成。 not least because 表示“尤其因为”。result in 导致

---

### Q49. The volatile chemicals produced by plants can be carried a long way, and they are very characteristic: Each plant or tree has its own particular odor signature.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句由两个分句构成， 分句之间用and连接。冒号后面的内容是对characteristic的解释说明。produced by plants是后置定语，修饰chemicals。

---

### Q50. The experiment showed that elephants may well use smell to identify patches of trees that are good to eat, and secondly to assess the quality of the trees within each patch.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句主干为The experiment showed that...，that引导宾语从句，从句中，use smell to identify...and to assess...是use...to do...结构，表示“用…来做…”。patches of trees that are good to eat，that are good to eat是定语从句，修饰patches of trees
