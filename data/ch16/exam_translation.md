# Ch. — 翻译 (Translation)

> **年份**: 2014 | **总分**: 10分

---

### Q46. It is also the reason why when we try to describe music with words, all we can do is articulate our reactions to it, and not grasp music itself.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句的重点在于理解“articulate our reactions”和“grasp music itself”之间的对比。前者是我们可以做到的，后者是我们无法做到的，这种对比体现了音乐的抽象性。翻译时注意“articulate”可以根据语境翻译为“表达”或“说清楚”。

---

### Q47. By all accounts he was a freethinking person, and a courageous one, and I find courage an essential quality for the understanding, let alone the performance, of his works.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句包含一个固定短语“By all accounts”，意为“总而言之”。后半句的重点在于理解“let alone”的用法，表示“更不用说”。翻译时注意语气的递进关系，即勇气对于理解作品是重要的，但对于演奏作品来说则更为重要。

---

### Q48. Beethoven’s habit of increasing the volume with an extreme intensity and then abruptly following it with a sudden soft passage was only rarely used by composers before him.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句是一个长句，主语是“Beethoven’s habit”，后面是由“increasing…and…following…”构成的动名词短语作后置定语。谓语是“was used”。翻译时可以采用拆分法，将长句拆分成几个短句，使表达更为流畅。

---

### Q49. Especially significant was his view of freedom, which, for him, was associated with the rights and responsibilities of the individual: he advocated freedom of thought and of personal expression.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句采用倒装结构，将表语“Especially significant”置于句首，以示强调。翻译时需要将其调整为正常语序。后半句是对“freedom”的解释，可以采用顺译法。

---

### Q50. One could interpret much of the work of Beethoven by saying that suffering is inevitable, but the courage to fight it renders life worth living.

**参考译文/解析**: 本句的主干是“One could interpret…by saying that…”。“that”引导的是一个宾语从句，其中“suffering is inevitable”和“the courage to fight it renders life worth living”是两个并列的分句。翻译时需要理清句子结构，并注意“render”的用法，表示“使…变得”。
